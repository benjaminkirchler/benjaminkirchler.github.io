Instructions

How the task works

  1. On the next page, you will see a field containing 100 boxes.
  2. Each box you select earns 1 token. Each token is worth €0.10.
  3. If any selected box contains a bomb, you lose all earnings from that round.
  4. Select carefully: once you select a box, you cannot unselect it.
  5. When you have selected all the boxes you want, click Collect money.
  6. You will receive no outcome feedback during play. After all three rounds, the results show how many bombs were hidden and whether you selected one.
  7. At the end, one of the three rounds is randomly selected for payment. Your final bank account equals the earnings from that round only.

About this task

Objective
Click boxes on a 10×10 grid to collect money. One box hides a bomb. Decide when to stop and collect — if you click the bomb, you earn nothing that round.
What it measures
Risk preferences under uncertainty. The number of boxes opened measures willingness to take on increasing risk for higher potential payoffs.
Optimal strategy
Open exactly 50 boxes to maximise expected earnings (£2.50/round on average). Opening more than 50 boxes reduces your expected payoff.
Payoff formula
E(n) = n × £0.10 × (100 − n) / 100 — an inverted U peaking at n = 50 (£2.50/round expected).
Probability structure
1 bomb hidden uniformly at random in 100 boxes. Conditional bomb probability on click n = 1 / (101 − n), increasing with every click.
