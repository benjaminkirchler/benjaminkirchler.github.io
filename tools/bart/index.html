Interactive demo  ·  Lejuez et al. (2002)  ·  3 rounds

Each pump earns £0.05 added to a temporary reserve. Click Collect money to bank it permanently. If the balloon bursts, the temporary reserve is lost. The balloon can burst on any pump — even the first one.

How the probability works: Each round a secret break point is drawn from a uniform distribution. Your burst risk on pump n is 1 ÷ (32 − n) — it starts at ~3% and rises with every pump. Each round is independent: surviving 15 pumps in round 1 tells you nothing about round 2.

What this measures: The adjusted BART score (average pumps on non-burst rounds) is a validated behavioural measure of risk-taking propensity correlated with sensation seeking, impulsivity, and real-world risk behaviours including substance use (Lejuez et al., 2002). The burst mechanism uses sampling without replacement — each pump draws from a shrinking pool, so conditional burst probability increases with every pump. This mirrors real-world situations where excessive risk produces diminishing returns and rising danger. In the original study, the temporary reserve amount is deliberately hidden from participants.
