What this measures: Your average number of pumps on non-burst rounds (the "adjusted BART score") is a validated measure of risk-taking propensity. Higher scores indicate greater willingness to accept risk for larger potential rewards. The task is widely used in behavioural economics and clinical psychology to elicit individual risk preferences.
